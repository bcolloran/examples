Company.com Usage Details:
Understanding Long-Term Trends

This dashboard is designed to help you better understand the health of Company.com by presenting the Data Team's estimates of the trend in Active Daily User (ADU) counts. In addition to understanding these trends in ADU, you can also learn more about the cyclical patterns in the use of Company.com, and anomalies in our data collection pipeline.

ADU components

Company.com ADU can be broken down into five components. By clicking the buttons above the graph, you can see these components individually or in whatever combination you wish. Each component is described in detail below.

Long-Term Trend

The most important component of the ADU signal for understanding the health of Company.com is the long-term trend in daily activity, measured as the count of daily Pings expected in the absence of other variation. This deseasonalized and denoised trend peaked in late 2013.

Seasonality

There are clear annual patterns in Company.com usage, the most important of which are the "summer slump", a slow decline which runs roughly from May to September, and sharp decline in activity around the Christmas and New Year's holidays.

The seasonal component of variation is measured as multiple of the trend.

Weekly Variation

Company.com usage is heavily concentrated during the workweek, peaking on Tuesday and reaching its minimum on Sunday. Interestingly the weekly variation has increased over time: in late 2008, the weekends lows had 79% of the activity weekday peaks in 2015.

The weekly component of variation is measured as multiple of the trend.

Outliers

This data contains a number of outliers, which typically represent problems with the data collection pipeline (server or database outages). The frequency of these outages peaked in early 2014, leading IT to update the pipeline, and prompting the Data Team to adopt more sophisticated outlier detection techniques. We are now able to detect outliers and filter them out of the data, which prevents them from affecting our estimates of the long-term trend and other components.

The outlier component of variation is measured as multiple of the trend.

Stochastic Noise

As with all statistical models, this decomposition includes random noise that cannot be attributed to the other components of variation.

The noise component of variation is measured as multiple of the trend.

Key things to know about this decomposition

This decomposition is what economists refer to as a "nowcast", and as such, it is subject to revision as new data comes in. Importantly, the details of the trend estimate for the most recent 12-18 months are likely to change. The seasonal and weekly component are somewhat more stable, but they also undergo continuous revision.

This decomposition makes a few simplifying assumptions, including (among others):

  • treating the seasonal variation as consistent across years;

  • limiting the resolution of the seasonal features considered;

  • treating the weekly variation as consistent across weeks, but with linear drift (increasing variation over time);

  • limiting the rate at which the underlying trend can change.

How to understand the forecasts

In the "Options" menu above the plots, you may choose to display forecasts over two time horizons:

  1. from January first of this year to the end of the year

  2. from today's date to the end of the year

The first forecast shows expectations at the time annual goals were set, and by comparing that with the second forecast, it is possible to evaluate performance against target.

How to read the growth plot

The bottom plot shows the estimated change of the value depicted in the top plot. From the options menu, you may choose to show this rate as either a percentage change or plain value. You may also choose to show the daily change or you can show the annualized daily change, which shows what the change would be if the rate of change on that day were continued for 365 days.

Please note that most cases, you will probably wish to view this graph with only the trend component active, because that will give you the current long-term rate growth without including cyclical components.
